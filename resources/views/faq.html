FAQ (CO-OP MEMBERSHIP)

  1. What's a cooperative?
    Co-operatives are owned and democratically controlled by their own members, who decide on the social mission and business model. (Ref: http://www.sncf.coop/)
  2. Are the committe paid?
    No, it’s a volunteer committee. The club will engage part-time staff to assist in its operation.
  3. How is the club funded?
    The Singapore National Co-operative Federation may provide seed funding for Runninghour to grow its business capability on a co-funding and reimbursement basis.
  4. How do I become a RH member?
    Members will be shareholders of the co-operative.
  5. How much is the RH share?
    Members need to purchase at least 1 share at SGD$10.00 per share.
  6. Do shareholders receive dividends?
    This is a co-operative that will not issue dividends.
  7. Is the RH share capital refundable?
    Yes, share capital is refundable upon resignation.

RUNNING GUIDE

  1. What is the commitment level?
    Once a week on a Saturday morning for about 2-3 hours.  Once a week for weekday for about 2 hours.
  2. What if I can’t commit on Saturday?
    We understand that you may have your own commitment, hence the commitment need not be every week, but do inform the available weeks that you can commit.
  3. Will I affect the special members?
    No, you will not affect members but regular commitment will help build bonds between members and guides
  4. How long do I need to commit?
    For as long as you are available.
  5. What is the duration of commitment?
    2-3 hours per session.
  6. How can you make a difference?
    By running and let your presence felt and by encouraging our members will make a difference in their running regime.
  7. How can you make a difference as a running guide?
    By volunteering your valuable time with us.
  8. What are my roles and responsibilities?
    To run as a buddy to both the Intellectually and Visually challenged runners, to look after their safety during outdoor runs and lastly to encourage them in their runs or races.
  9. What are the dos and don’ts for guiding the visually challenged runners?
    Dos
    Encourage
    Take time to build bond / friendship with them
    Share your running experience if you do have
    Look after their safety during runs
    Run together / along with their pace
    Allow them to decide on the timing if they are running for races but as a sighted guide,
    you can help share and guide them along if you find their expectations not realistic
    Don'ts
    Scold or use remarks that are not helpful
    Use long sentences
    Use big and bombastic words
  10. What are the skills needed to be a good running guide?
    Take time to understand the person you are paired with as everyone is different. Allow yourself to be open and ready to learn from each other. Be alert and observant in the surrounding especially you are guiding VCR so that you can be alert to warn them of any danger. Uses of the sense in term of touch e.g.  Allow the VCR to run holding your shoulder or elbow helps in eliminating the fear and feels safe.
  11. What should I take note of during races?
    Safety is our utmost important. Look out for the runner’s health before run and if they are not feeling well, do modify the pace according to the fitness / health of the runners.
  12. How do I determine how long the towel / laces be?
    It depends. In a race and crowded outdoor running places, we try to keep it short. In a spacious area, it is fine to keep it a bit longer.
  13. When do I start telling them on some obstacles ahead?
    It depends on the visually runners you are guiding, some is fine with you alerting them at a distance but some prefer to be inform when nearer the obstacle. However in a race, it will be good to inform slightly earlier as the running pace can be fast.
  14. Can I terminate the volunteering service?
    Yes, you can at any time. All you have to do is to inform us.
    If you bought shares as our Co-op member, all we do is to refund you the shares amount when you leave the co-op.
